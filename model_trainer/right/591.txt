Kelly Clarkson’s Ex-Husband Brandon Blackstock Dies At 48
Entertainment Kelly Clarkson’s Ex-Husband Brandon Blackstock Dies At 48 (Photo by Frazer Harrison/Getty Images) Leena Nasir Entertainment Reporter August 07, 2025 2:39 PM ET August 07, 2025 2:39 PM ET Leena Nasir Entertainment Reporter Font Size: Brandon Blackstock, music manager and ex-husband of singer Kelly Clarkson, died of cancer Thursday at 48, his family said. “Brandon bravely battled cancer for more than three years,” a family spokesperson told People. “He passed away peacefully and was surrounded by family. We thank you for your thoughts and prayers and ask everyone to respect the family’s privacy during this very difficult time.” (RELATED: Kelly Clarkson Cancels All August Performances At Las Vegas Residency) NEW YORK, NY – JANUARY 28: Brandon Blackstock and recording artist Kelly Clarkson attend the 60th Annual GRAMMY Awards at Madison Square Garden on January 28, 2018 in New York City. (Photo by Christopher Polk/Getty Images for NARAS) Blackstock, a longtime talent manager, was also the former stepson of country star Reba McEntire. Clarkson revealed his illness in an Instagram post Wednesday announcing the postponement of her Las Vegas residency dates. “While I normally keep my personal life private, this past year, my children’s father has been ill and at this moment, I need to be fully present for them,” she wrote. (Photo by Michael Buckner/Getty Images for Celebrity Fight Night) Clarkson did not disclose Blackstock’s cancer diagnosis at the time. He had four children — Savannah and Seth from a previous relationship, and River and Remington with Clarkson. The couple finalized a contentious divorce in 2022.
