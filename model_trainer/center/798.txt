Superman actor says he is joining ICE 'ASAP' in bid to 'save America'
Ex-Superman Dean Cain to join ICE 'ASAP' to 'save America' Cain, 59 and who was cast as Superman in the TV series Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman, announced he had joined the team amid the federal agency's unprecedented immigration raids. Claire Gilbody-Dickerson Reporter @ClaireGilbodyD Thursday 7 August 2025 14:06, UK Image: Dean Cain as Superman. Pic: Warner Bros/Everett/Shutterstock Why you can trust Sky News Dean Cain has been branded the "worst superman ever" as he announced he will join the US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) "ASAP". The 59-year-old, who was cast as Superman in the TV series Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman, announced he had joined the team amid the federal agency's unprecedented immigration raids. He told Fox News' Jesse Watters Primetime show on Wednesday his recruitment video on Instagram had gone viral and since then, "I have spoken with some of the officials over at ICE and I will be sworn in as an ICE agent ASAP". "You can defend your homeland and get great benefits," he said in the Instagram post where he appealed for his followers to join ICE.
